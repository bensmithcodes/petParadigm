Around 7500 BCE in the western Asia region, cats were domesticated, and they are now the second most popular domestic pet in the United States. Even though there are over 60 documented cat breeds, the care and diet required can vary between breeds. However, certain diets and practices are commonly recommended for the overall health of your furry family member, regardless of breed.

Zero to Four Weeks

Ensuring the queen (a commonly used term for a female cat that is pregnant or nursing) directly nurses her young is crucial. It is essential to monitor your kitten's growth closely to ensure that it is growing at a steady rate. If any kitten is not growing sufficiently, a caretaker might need to feed the kitten directly, either with a bottle or a feeding tube. Some reasons why kittens may not gain weight appropriately include:

  • Too many other siblings are competing for mom's milk
  • Gastrointestinal disease
  • Environmental conditions such as extreme heat or cold, or unsanitary conditions

Four Weeks to One Year

Kittens can start being introduced to soft wet kitten food typically around three to four weeks after birth. According to the ASPCA,At this stage, it's recommended for kittens to consume half to one cup of dry kitten food or six to nine ounces of wet kitten food per day. If your kitten has difficulty eating hard food, you can add a small amount of water to soften it. Specialized kitten food provides the extra nutrients required for their growth, energy, and well-being. However, depending on the breed, your cat may have different dietary needs, so it's best to consult your veterinarian for advice.

One Year to Seven Years

During the cat stage, which typically starts when kittens reach a certain age, their need for nutrients decreases. Kittens become less active, and their metabolism slows down. It's not advisable to leave food out for your cats all day long. Instead, feed them a few times a day, so they have meals rather than snacks throughout the day. This routine helps prevent obesity and other weight-related health issues in felines.

Seven Years and More

Similar to other living beings, as cats age, their bodies undergo changes and start to deteriorate. At this stage, it is advisable to feed cats with lesser fats and calories and more quality proteins. When purchasing cat food, it is recommended to opt for food that specifies a particular protein, such as "salmon," rather than just a category like "fish." The latter usually indicates byproducts or combinations of lower quality proteins.
