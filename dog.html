STOP RESOURCE FOR HEALTHY AND HAPPY PETS

With over 300 breeds recognized by the World Canine Organization, the domestic dog is an incredibly social animal, offering a vast array of choices for potential owners. Adopting a dog can be beneficial for both the dog and its owner, with studies highlighting the social benefits of companionship and reduced feelings of loneliness. Additionally, research has shown that dog ownership can lead to improved medical indicators such as reduced blood pressure and improved lipid profiles. To ensure that your furry companion remains healthy, it is important to provide them with proper care, including a balanced diet and regular exercise. On this page, you can find more information on how to care for your pet throughout the various stages of their life.

Diet & Exercise


As dogs age, their feeding schedule should be adjusted accordingly, with a gradual reduction in meal frequency. Puppies should be nursed for the first two months, and then transitioned to three meals per day from three to six months of age. From six months to one year, their meals should be reduced to two per day, and finally, to one meal per day after one year of age. Dogs typically have a body mass consisting of 60 to 70 percent water. Even a minor reduction of 10 percent in body water can cause illness, while a loss of 15 percent can lead to death. Thus, it is crucial to ensure that your pet stays hydrated. Similar to humans, dogs also require a sufficient level of activity to maintain healthy organs, in addition to exposure to sunlight and fresh air. The minimum recommended exercise time can vary significantly based on breed and size, but it is generally recommended that dogs receive 30 to 120 minutes of exercise per day. However, some may find the idea of exercising their dog for two hours a day unappealing, so it is important to choose activities that are suitable for you and your pet. Brisk walks and age-appropriate toys are great ways to encourage physical activity, but be mindful of toys that require supervision to avoid posing a choking hazard to your furry friend.

Grooming & Handling


Maintaining your furry companion's cleanliness is crucial for their overall health. Regular brushing can significantly reduce shedding and prevent matted fur that can be difficult to remove. It's also important to check for ticks and fleas, particularly after visits to heavily wooded areas. Bathing is another essential aspect of pet care, but it can also be a workout, especially with more resistant dogs. When bathing your dog, it's crucial to rinse off all the soap to prevent skin irritations. Additionally, ensure that the soap is suitable for your dog's breed, as harsh soaps can cause allergic reactions. Lastly, it's crucial to handle your furry friend with care, regardless of its size. For smaller dogs, cradle the pup with one hand under the chest and the forearm supporting the back half. For larger dogs, lift them by reaching under their belly with both arms perpendicular to the dog and using each arm to support the chest and rear.

Vaccinations & Medications


You should always It's advisable to seek guidance from your veterinarian, as there is a standard set of vaccines that are commonly recommended. These vaccines provide protection against diseases such as hepatitis, rabies, and parvovirus, which can pose significant health risks to your dog. Additional vaccinations may be recommended based on your dog's living conditions and surroundings, and your vet can provide specific recommendations for your pet Bordetella bronchiseptica, Borrelia burgdorferi, and Leptospira bacteria.
