Fish are a diverse group of aquatic animals that can thrive in both freshwater and saltwater environments, depending on their species. Introducing fish into your household can be a thrilling hobby, particularly if you plan to breed them. Additionally, having an aquarium in your home can promote a sense of calm and serenity.

Testing your aquarium water is crucial whether you are introducing new fish or maintaining an existing aquarium. once per week to make sure it is free from toxins that can harm your fish.

Freshwater Fish

When setting up your freshwater aquarium, it's best to consult with a "Pexpert" to ensure that your fish will get along with each other. Make sure that the fish species you choose are suitable for a freshwater environment by using a water testing kit to check the water chemistry. Follow these general guidelines to help your fish adapt well to their new environment and avoid toxins that can harm or even kill them:

  • pH Level: 6.8–7.6 (7.0 is considered neutral)
  • Ammonia: any level above zero is harmful to fish
  • Nitrates: 5 to 10 ppm
  • Nitrites: less than 0.5 ppm

Saltwater Fish

When it comes to selecting saltwater fish for your aquarium, there are numerous species to choose from. However, it's essential to consult with a "Pexpert" to ensure that your fish will coexist and thrive in a saltwater environment. To avoid hazardous water conditions and maintain the right salinity level for your fish, follow these general guidelines.:
  • Salinity: 1.020–1.028 sg
  • pH Level: 7.6–8.4 (7.0 is considered neutral)
  • Ammonia: any level above 0.1 ppm requires attention
  • Nitrates: 10 to 40 ppm
  • Nitrates: less than 0.2 ppm
